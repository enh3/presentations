1. Contexte de l'hyper-instrument

    Qu'est-ce qu'un hyper-instrument?

      « Well, the basic idea of a hyperinstrument is where the technology is built right into the instrument so that the instrument knows how it's being played literally, what the expression is, what the meaning is, what the direction of the music is. » - Todd Machover, 2008.

    • Distingué de la luthérie numérique par l'utilisation des instruments acoustiques.

1.1 Defining Contradiction

First Thesis: Contradiction doesn’t negate meaning. It is precisely there that meaning lives.

"For the law of contradiction is the most fundamental logical law said to be at the basis of all intelligible speech, thought, argumentative practice, and action, without which all speech and thought would dis- integrate into unstable, incoherent, nonsensical verbal constructions."
Aristotle, Metaphysics.

"Contradiction is the rule for the truth, noncontradiction for falsehood."
Heraclitus, Habilitationsthese I.

"Everything stands in opposition."
Hegel, Science of Logic.

1.2 Why care?

Second Thesis: The cosmology of the artist; her perspective of herself and how she relates to the world around her, is foundational to her work and to her vision.

"THE QUALITY OF LIGHT by which we scrutinize our lives has direct bearing upon the product which we live, and upon the changes which we hope to bring about through those lives. It is within this light that we form those ideas by which we pursue our magic and make it realized. This is poetry as illumination, for it is through poetry that we give name to those ideas which are - until the poem - nameless and formless…"
Audre Lorde, Sister Outsider.

2. Projects

2.1 Ad viger

  • Compiled over the summer of 2014
  • Inspiration
    • The memory of hearing Boards of Canada for the first time while driving back from work from a night shift in high school (hauntology).
    • A fantasy of inhabiting the family home, after having lost every person that I’ve ever known or loved, seeing the vacant empty space, which has been transformed into ruin, and being flooded with the memories of the joy of life.
  • All the things you aren't
    • First track on album
    • Reference to the song All the Things You Are by Jerome Kern
    • The negation points to two contradictions:
      • The I vs l'ensemble
      • Imagined vs real

2.2 Enjoy ur Sunl3ss Endeavours

  • Released in July of 2017
  • Inspiration
    • Title comes from a text I sent my mother while working at a fake bakery.
    • I wanted to make an album inspired by the day, and the sun, as opposed to my first album which was very much nocturnal in my mind. But it’s not so much taking place outside. It’s from the perspective of a room.
    • Drawing by Erik Satie


  • Feminism: the personal is political

    • “It is this voicing of the personal (the “inner world”) which constitutes a political act, the raising of a voice that traditionally has not been raised, has been shrouded in silence."
    Hildegard Westerkamp, Listening and Soundmaking: A Study of Music-As-Environment.

    "As a whole generation of men, we are so estranged from the inner world that there are many arguing that it does not exist."
    Westerkamp quoting R.D. Laing.

  • Nihilism: meaning is not inherent
    • Keiji Haino - I Said, This Is The Son Of Nihilism
    • Death Grips - Eh
    • Boards of Canada - Everything you do is a Balloon
  • Self deprecation and the liar paradox
  • Mushroom's Day Off
    • Seventh track on the album. From the perspective of Erik Satie as a mushroom living inside his piano in his apartment in Arcueil, pondering the existential.

2.4 Prière(return)

  • Foundational question: what if we consider the inverse of being not as nothingness but as becoming?
  • Kafka's Metamorphosis
  • Gödel's hole
  • Symbols

    • Visual: The closed eye, and green-blue planes traversing a void (made in Blender)
